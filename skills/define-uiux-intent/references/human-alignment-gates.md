# Human Alignment Gates

Use gates to keep AI design work aligned without asking too many questions.

## Gate 1: Intent Lock

Stop when the product purpose, audience, task, or success criteria are ambiguous enough to change the design. Ask the smallest set of questions that resolves the direction.

Proceed without asking only when:

- Existing product context answers the question.
- The user requested speed.
- A conservative default is obvious and explicitly stated.

## Gate 2: Direction Choice

Offer 2-3 directions that differ by system model, flow, or information hierarchy. Include tradeoffs and a recommendation.

Do not offer cosmetic-only options such as "blue", "purple", or "minimal" unless brand exploration is the actual task.

## Gate 3: Rendered Reality

After implementation or prototype work, use a screenshot, browser view, or running local UI for review when possible. Ask for human judgment on taste, brand fit, emotional tone, and whether the product feels right for its audience.

## Gate 4: Interaction Feel

For motion, AI agents, or complex workflows, align on the interaction feedback map before treating the design as ready.

Ask what the user should feel at critical moments: control, trust, speed, calm, momentum, precision, or delight.
