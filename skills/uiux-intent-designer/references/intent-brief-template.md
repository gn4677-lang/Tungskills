# Intent Brief Template

Use this when UI/UX intent is incomplete or when the task affects a major surface.

## Minimum Brief

- Product type:
- Primary audience:
- Primary task:
- User context:
- Success criteria:
- Constraints:
- Tone and trust posture:
- Density target:
- Primary action:
- Secondary actions:
- Non-goals:
- Human judgment still needed:

## Intent Questions

Ask only questions that would change the design. Prefer concrete choices.

- Audience: Who must understand this immediately?
- Task: What should they be able to do without reading instructions?
- Trust: What proof, evidence, or system feedback makes the experience credible?
- Density: Should this feel fast and operational, guided and calm, or expressive and exploratory?
- Risk: What mistake would be most expensive or frustrating?
- Brand: Should the UI feel restrained, technical, warm, playful, editorial, premium, or utilitarian?

## Defaults When Speed Matters

If the user asks to move fast and context is thin:

- Pick a conservative product-appropriate pattern.
- State assumptions before implementation.
- Avoid decorative hero layouts for tools and operational products.
- Prefer clear literal copy over abstract marketing language.
- Leave brand taste as a human review item.

## Landing Page Defaults

For broad landing-page requests, default to:

- Audience-first hero with a literal subheadline.
- Product screenshot, workflow preview, or concrete output above generic benefit cards.
- Primary CTA based on product maturity: `Book a demo` for enterprise, `Start free` for self-serve, `Join waitlist` for prelaunch.
- Trust proof near the moment of doubt: source links, auditability, customer logos, security, examples, or benchmark evidence.
- One coherent visual system instead of generic AI gradients and decorative cards.
