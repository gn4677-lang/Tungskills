# Objective Quality Checklist

Use this to catch problems that do not require taste judgment.

## Rendered Layout

- Text is readable and not clipped.
- Buttons and inputs keep stable dimensions.
- No incoherent overlap between text, controls, cards, modals, nav, or media.
- Content remains usable at mobile, tablet, and desktop widths.
- Fixed-format elements have stable dimensions.
- Primary action is visible where the task needs it.

## Interaction States

- Hover states clarify affordance.
- Focus state is visible and not obscured.
- Active and selected states are distinct.
- Disabled states explain or imply why action is unavailable.
- Loading states preserve layout and communicate scope.
- Empty states tell users what can happen next.
- Error states are specific and recoverable.
- Success states confirm the result.

## Accessibility Basics

- Keyboard path reaches important controls.
- Headings and labels describe purpose.
- Text and controls have sufficient contrast.
- Target sizes are usable.
- Motion respects reduced-motion preferences.
- Information is not conveyed only by color, animation, or position.

## Content Clarity

- First screen explains what this is.
- Copy speaks to the target user.
- Labels use domain terms only when the audience would understand them.
- CTAs say what happens next.
- Demo data looks credible and relevant.
